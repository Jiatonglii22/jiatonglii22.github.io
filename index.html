My Class Projects

Color Puzzle Game


I coded this game in Python and was inspired by the mobile game I Love Hue. I'm very drawn to aesthetically pleasing colors and wanted to create something that replicated the same feel while incorporating complexity in generating different shapes.

Grid of regular squares

Start of game

Grid of isometric squares

Start of game

Grid of randomly generated polygons

Start of game


The game has three modes, and the player is presented with either a grid of regular squares, isometric sqaures, or randomly generated polygons using Voronoi's Algorithm. The color gradient is also randomly generated for each game. The goal of the game is to rebuild the gradient by switching different colors around on the grid. A hint feature is included for whenever the player needs assistence.

OpenTable Database Project

Completed with a partner

OpenTable is a restaurant reservation service and the purpose of the project was to design how its database could be implemented to reflect the actual services in OpenTable. This project allowed me to explore the life cycle of database development, from understanding the business model and identifying user stories to constructing physical models with Vertabelo and writing code in plpgsql to realize the functions of the database.

Working with a partner meant that I was encouraged to communicate my ideas clearly so that we could reach a conclusion that we're both satisfied with. Since the project was very open ended, we often had to discuss many details and look over each other's work to give feedback.

Our final deliverable can be viewed in Github.


Marble Puzzle Box (made for kids)

Completed as a group

I learned how to use SolidWorks and laser cutters to help bring this project to life. Our group's idea was to create a box in which the marbles inside can be moved around with magnets. One of the biggest lessons I learned from this project was that the development of anything is an iterative process. We ended up making many changes to our original design after learning from our mistakes and testing our prototypes.

My Hobby Projects

ART


Art has always been one of my creative outlets. I started off doing traditional art with color pencils and acrylic paints but in high school, I discovered that I enjoy creating 3D art so much more. I'm fascinated by how art can be expressed in 3 dimensions with mixed media and how it can portray a different message when viewed from a different angle. My motivation as an artist is to create artworks that captivate my viewers and encourage them to take the time to examine my work from all angles so that they can walk away with them their own interpretation of my artwork.

A more complete collection of my artworks can be found in this Instagram account.

About Me

Hello! Thank you for stopping by :)

My name is Jiatong Li and I am a sophmore at Carnegie Mellon University studying Information Systems.

My interest in the application of technology in education stems from my own desire to teach myself different languages and skills, and a deep fascination for how the brain adapts and learns. As technology becomes more widespread, I'm curious as to how it can improve the quality and speed of how we learn, in both educational systems and independent individuals. I find that a major in Information Systems gives me the flexibility to explore different applications of technology and challenges me to rethink how it can shape our society.

At my core, I am a creator, and I consistently seek out new ways to express myself creatively. I'm part of the open rehearsal acapella group, C++, and StreetStyle, a freestyle dance club on campus. Below are some links where you can watch some of the performances that I was in!
